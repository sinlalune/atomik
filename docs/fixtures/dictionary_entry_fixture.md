# philosophy

**Language:** English  
**Part of speech:** noun  
**Pronunciation:** source-dependent; see evidence drawer for regional variants

## Senses

1. The systematic study of fundamental questions concerning existence, knowledge, reason, value, mind, and language.
2. A system or set of guiding principles associated with a person, school, activity, or practice.

> **Entry status:** source-linked baseline. Definitions are concise summaries and may not cover every historical or specialist use.

## Etymology

English **philosophy** entered through earlier French and Latin transmission from Ancient Greek *philosophía*, conventionally analyzed from elements glossed as “love” and “wisdom.”

```text
Ancient Greek philosophía
  -> Latin philosophia
  -> Old/Middle French transmission
  -> English philosophy
```

> **Etymology status:** broadly supported summary · transmission simplified
